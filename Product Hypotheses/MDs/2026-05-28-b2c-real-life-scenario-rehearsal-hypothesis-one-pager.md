# AI Simulation For Real-Life Rehearsal

## Hero Description

Turn a real-life situation into an interactive AI simulation that people can rehearse before it happens.

The value is a grounded practice loop, not generic AI chat: users set the people, context, and stakes, then run realistic simulations, retry them, and get feedback that helps them perform better in the real moment.

## Internal Justification

AI roleplay is now credible, but most products still optimize for business coaching, sales readiness, or generic conversation practice. Very few products are built around simulating the user's actual situation with enough context to make rehearsal feel specific, repeatable, and emotionally useful.

That creates room for a workflow-first product that combines scenario setup, multi-character simulation, retries, and feedback in one loop. The wedge is not “talk to an AI character.” It is “practice the real interaction before it happens,” especially when the situation is social, tense, or hard to improvise live.

## ICP

People who want to prepare for real conversations and perform better in context, not just receive advice.

Best-fit early users:

- users preparing for difficult family, partner, or roommate conversations
- people with anxiety around upcoming social or practical interactions
- job seekers practicing interviews, including panel interviews
- language learners who want realistic situational speaking practice
- users who already use AI for reflection, coaching, or message drafting

## Use Case / User Story

### Difficult Conversation At Home

Input:
user describes a conversation with their partner about finances, names the likely triggers, and uploads a photo of the kitchen where the talk will happen

System does:
creates a lightweight scene, generates a partner persona with the requested behavior patterns, simulates likely reactions, and lets the user retry the discussion with different phrasing or difficulty levels

Output:
a grounded roleplay with realistic pushback, repeat practice, and feedback on tone, escalation, clarity, and empathy

User outcome:
the user becomes less anxious, finds language that feels natural, and enters the real conversation more prepared

### Panel Interview Preparation

Input:
user selects a job interview scenario, describes the role and company, and defines three interviewers with different personalities and goals

System does:
creates a multi-person simulation, manages turn-taking and interruptions, tests how the user handles different stakeholders, and scores answers across clarity, confidence, and coherence

Output:
a replayable interview simulation with several interviewers, adjustable difficulty, and structured feedback after each run

User outcome:
the user improves readiness for high-pressure interviews and gets practice that feels closer to the real room than one-on-one chatbot rehearsal

## Key Competitors To Watch And Why

Using the existing competitor notes as the research source, the closest references are:

- [Yoodli](https://yoodli.ai/): strongest benchmark for realistic roleplay plus immediate coaching, analytics, and retry loops across interviews, leadership, and other high-stakes conversations
- [Second Nature](https://secondnature.ai/): strong reference for avatar-based roleplay, scalable feedback, and turning conversation practice into operational readiness infrastructure
- [Virti](https://www.virti.com/): important signal for immersive scenario delivery, virtual humans, and packaging conversation simulation inside a broader experiential learning platform
- [Mursion](https://www.mursion.com/): best benchmark for premium high-stakes simulations where believable practice, coaching, and behavior change matter more than lightweight chat
- [DialogueTrainer](https://www.dialoguetrainer.com/): useful reference for difficult-conversation rehearsal, realistic virtual actors, and measurable soft-skills improvement

## Key Features

### MVP

- user creates a scenario by describing the people, stakes, and goal
- user uploads a photo of the setting or selects a similar environment
- Dexi generates a lightweight scene and one or more relevant characters
- the simulation supports voice or voice-plus-text interaction
- the user can retry the same scenario with different reactions or difficulty
- the system gives post-session feedback on clarity, tone, confidence, and escalation handling

### Potential extension

- multi-character scenes as a default mode, not just a single AI counterpart
- persistent scenario libraries such as `my boss`, `my partner`, or `my interview panel`
- scene editing to change environment, mood, or who is present
- language-learning variants with accent, pace, and formality controls

## Success Hypothesis

We should consider the hypothesis promising if early users consistently say:

- "This feels close to my real situation."
- "This helped me prepare what to say."
- "This is more useful than generic AI chat."
- "I want to retry the same scenario in different ways."
- "This made the real conversation feel less scary."
