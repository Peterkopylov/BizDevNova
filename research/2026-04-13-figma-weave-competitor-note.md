# Figma Weave Competitor Note

## Summary

Figma Weave is a standalone AI-native media creation platform, built from Figma's acquisition of Weavy, that combines multiple top image, video, and 3D models with editing tools inside a node-based workflow canvas.

It matters because it is not selling just `AI generation`. It is selling:

- multi-model creative workflows
- professional editing inside the same canvas
- production-grade team control
- enterprise-safe adoption of generative media

## Why It Matters

Figma Weave is one of the clearest `workflow-first` competitors in the market.

It validates demand for:

- model aggregation
- visual workflow building
- image, video, and 3D in one tool
- team-ready production control
- commercial-rights and enterprise packaging

This makes it more strategically relevant than a pure image generator.

## Pricing Signals

### Public Pricing

Figma Weave publishes official pricing:

- `Free`: `$0/month`, `150 monthly credits`, `5 workflows`
- `Starter`: `$24/month` billed annually as `$228`, `1,500 monthly credits`
- `Professional`: `$45/month` billed annually as `$432`, `4,000 monthly credits`
- `Team`: `$60/user/month` billed annually as `$576`, `4,500 monthly credits/user`
- `Enterprise`: custom

### Included Signals

- `Free` includes full access to AI models, editing tools, commercial license, but only `5 workflows`
- `Starter` unlocks unlimited workflows and top-ups at `$10 for 1,000 credits`
- `Professional` adds `3-month credit rollover` and top-ups at `$10 for 1,200 credits`
- `Team` adds shared credit pool, unified billing, and file sharing
- enterprise includes custom credit allocation, training, Slack support, own API keys, and future API workflows

## Valuation / Funding Signal

### Public Signal

The clearest public signal is the acquisition path:

- Weavy raised `$4M` seed funding in June 2025
- Figma acquired Weavy in October 2025
- valuation of the deal was not officially disclosed
- one market-data reference estimated the acquisition at `approximately $200M`

## Control / Consistency Relevance

Figma Weave is very relevant to our thesis because it strongly emphasizes:

- workflow-level control
- structure and references
- compositing and editing after generation
- reusable systems, not one-off prompting

It is one of the stronger proofs that the market values `control layer + production workflow`.

## GTM Insight

Figma Weave's GTM appears to combine:

1. creator and pro self-serve
2. team adoption through shared credits and workflow reuse
3. enterprise upsell through rights, governance, and support
4. distribution leverage from the broader Figma ecosystem

## Implications for Us

- this is a strong horizontal competitor for workflow-centric creation
- it validates that professional users care about more than model quality
- it raises the bar on node/workflow UX and enterprise packaging
- it does not yet prove dominance in deep still-image specialization, but it is highly credible as a broad platform threat

## Open Questions

- how quickly will Figma integrate Weave into the main Figma product?
- will the current standalone positioning persist or collapse into Figma AI as a feature set?
- how much real usage is image-first versus video-first versus 3D-first?
- can smaller specialists beat it on narrower vertical workflows despite Figma distribution?
