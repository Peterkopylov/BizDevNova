# Social Media Guideline: Sound Human, Not Like AI Slop

## Summary

One strong social media lesson:

- when everything starts sounding the same, being more human, more irreverent, and more scroll-stopping can become a major advantage

The core idea is that many audiences are tired of:

- guru-style posts
- fake authority
- formulaic success-post storytelling
- generic AI-polished writing

## Core Idea

Instead of sounding polished in the same way as everyone else, write like a real person.

That can include:

- calling out obvious nonsense
- saying the quiet part out loud
- using sharper language
- sounding less performative and more honest

## Why It Works

When the feed is full of repetitive, over-optimized content, audiences start filtering out anything that feels:

- templated
- preachy
- self-congratulatory
- unreal

A more human tone can create:

- attention
- trust
- curiosity
- stronger emotional reaction

## Observed Result

The reported outcome was:

- after switching to a more human and less guru-like voice, posts started performing much better
- some posts went viral after earlier posts had not

That suggests tone itself can be a major lever.

## Practical Lesson

People do not only want information.

They also want to feel that:

- a real person is talking
- the perspective is honest
- the content is not just another polished growth-performance script

## What to Avoid

Examples of content patterns people may now be tired of:

- `I did X and got absurd result Y in 2.5 hours`
- fake certainty
- exaggerated authority
- guru post structure
- empty high-performance language

## Better Direction

A stronger tone may sound:

- more direct
- more specific
- more skeptical
- more grounded
- more human

## Distribution Insight

Another important point from the discussion:

- post timing may matter somewhat
- but the impact can be marginal compared with stronger content signals

The more important drivers mentioned were:

- engagement, especially comments
- pause time
- topic relevance

## Hashtag Takeaway

The view expressed here is that hashtags are not as important as they used to be.

Instead, platforms seem to reward:

- whether people stop
- whether they stay
- whether they interact
- whether the content clearly maps to a topic

## Working Heuristic

If choosing where to focus effort, prioritize:

1. better hooks
2. stronger human voice
3. more comment-worthy ideas
4. content that increases pause time

Before spending too much energy on:

- hashtag optimization
- micro-timing tweaks

## Good Reminder

People want to hear from humans.

Not from content that sounds mass-produced, overly polished, or mechanically persuasive.
