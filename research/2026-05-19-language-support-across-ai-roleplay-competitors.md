# Language Support Across AI Roleplay Competitors

Date: 2026-05-19
Topic: Language and multilingual support across key AI roleplay competitors

## Summary

Language support is clearly part of the category, but competitors vary a lot in how explicitly they document it.

The strongest verified public signals in this pass are:

- `Skillsoft CAISY`: strongest count signal, with `40+ languages`
- `Yoodli`: strongest detailed list signal, with a broad published supported-language list
- `Mursion`: clear portal-language support list, but simulation delivery can vary by organization
- `Virti`: clear multilingual positioning, but no clean public language count surfaced in this pass
- `Second Nature`: clear multi-language support claim, but no clean official count surfaced in this pass
- `DialogueTrainer`: no strong public multilingual claim verified in this pass

### Mursion

Status:
- `clearly verified for portal UI; simulation-language availability is conditional`

What is publicly visible:
- Mursion support says the portal is available in `11 languages`:
  - English
  - French
  - French (Canada)
  - German
  - Spanish
  - Italian
  - Portuguese (Brazil)
  - Russian
  - Chinese (Simplified)
  - Japanese
  - Polish
- Mursion also notes that simulation delivery in each language may or may not be available for a given organization

Interpretation:
- good verified language support signal
- but the distinction between `portal language` and `actual scenario delivery language` matters

Sources:

## Practical Ranking

If we rank by the strength of public multilingual evidence:

1. `Skillsoft CAISY`
2. `Yoodli`
3. `Mursion`
4. `Virti`
5. `Second Nature`
6. `DialogueTrainer`

Important nuance:
- `Skillsoft` looks strongest on raw count
- `Yoodli` looks strongest on detailed roleplay-oriented language documentation
- `Mursion` has clear support documentation but with caveats
- `Virti` looks strategically multilingual, but with less precise public documentation in this pass

## Takeaway For Us

Language support already matters in this category, especially for:

- global sales training
- distributed customer support teams
- enterprise L&D rollouts
- education and workforce-skilling programs

If we want to look enterprise-ready, multilingual support is not a niche extra. It is increasingly part of the expected baseline, especially for larger accounts.
