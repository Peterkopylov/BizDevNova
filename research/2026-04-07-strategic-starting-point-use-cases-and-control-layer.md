# Strategic Starting Point: Use Cases and Control Layer

## Summary

Core framework for future business development research:

- do not look for places to "attach the engine"
- look for scenarios where the product is impossible or materially worse without the engine
- focus on use cases where the core value is `environment + action + consequence`
- explore not just `AI image generation`, but the product class of `layout-first content generation` / `control layer for generative AI`

Key strategic thesis:

> You are not a generator. You are the `editor of the world before it is rendered`.

## Context

This note summarizes an important ChatGPT exchange that defines two main exploration tracks:

1. `Simulation / interactive engine first` use cases
2. `Control layer before generation` as a potential product category

## Track 1: Use Cases Where the Engine Is Critical

### Selection Criteria

The idea filter is:

- without the engine, the product is impossible
- or without the engine, the product is materially worse

### Use Case 1: Pre-Sale Configurator for Real Estate

#### Who

- developers
- real estate builders
- real estate agencies

#### Pain

- apartments are sold from static visuals
- buyers struggle to understand the space
- buying decisions take longer

#### Solution

An interactive apartment with real-time customization:

- walk through the property
- change the layout
- change materials
- see the result instantly

#### Why There Is an Advantage

- requires real-time 3D
- requires interactivity
- requires fast scene assembly without engineering work

#### Monetization

- B2B SaaS
- project-based model
- high ticket: `EUR 5k-50k+` per project or subscription

#### Fast Test

- build one apartment demo
- create a landing page around `sell before build`
- do outreach to 20 developers

#### Risk

- this can collapse into an agency business
- productization discipline is essential

### Use Case 2: Interactive Walkthrough for Complex Products

#### Who

- B2B SaaS companies
- hardware companies
- industrial tech

#### Pain

- the product is hard to explain
- demos require sales involvement
- onboarding is heavy

#### Solution

An interactive "try it yourself" experience:

- not a video
- not a slide deck
- not a sales call
- but a hands-on interactive walkthrough

#### Why There Is an Advantage

- requires modeling a system, not just showing screens
- the user needs to act, not only watch
- scenario creation speed matters

#### Monetization

- value proposition tied to conversion uplift
- used in sales and onboarding

#### Fast Test

- pick one SaaS product
- build a fake interactive demo
- test whether it changes interest or conversion

#### Risk

- crowded space
- though many competitors are still limited to 2D and shallow interactivity

### Use Case 3: Safety / Operations Simulation

#### Who

- warehouses
- manufacturing
- logistics
- safety / compliance teams

#### Pain

- mistakes are expensive
- training is boring and ineffective
- real-world practice is unsafe or impossible

#### Solution

Action simulations inside an environment:

- what to do during an incident
- how to move through a warehouse
- what to press and in what order

#### Why There Is an Advantage

- environment, actions, and consequences matter
- text or video are poor substitutes
- this is a true `simulation-first` use case

#### Monetization

- training budgets
- compliance budgets
- enterprise contracts

#### Fast Test

- build one safety scenario
- show it to 5 companies
- ask whether they would pay for it

#### Risk

- long sales cycles
- enterprise complexity

## Shared Pattern Across the First 3 Use Cases

All strong candidates share:

- `environment`
- `action`
- `consequence`

### Comparison

| Use case | Is the engine required? | Revenue potential | Scalability |
|---|---|---|---|
| Real estate | Critical | High-ticket | Medium |

## Recommended Hypothesis Testing Approach

### Near-Term Plan

#### Weeks 1-2

- choose 2 of the 3 use cases
- build 2 demos

#### Weeks 3-4

- run 20 conversations
- run 5 tests

### Goal

Find the first use case with genuine `pull`, not something that must be pushed into the market.

### Kill Criterion

If after 20 conversations there is no strong reaction such as:

- "we need this"
- "this solves a real problem"
- "we would test or buy this"

then the hypothesis should be killed or radically reframed.

## Track 2: Control Layer Before Generation

## Core Idea

The user defines the structure of a scene first, and only then AI generates the final image on top of that structure.

Formula:

- not `prompt-first`
- but `layout-first`

Example:

- not "make it beautiful"
- but "here is where everything goes, now generate it beautifully"

## Why This Idea Could Be Strong

### 1. It Solves a Core Pain in AI Generation

It adds:

- control
- predictability
- repeatability
- editable iterations

### 2. It Natively Uses the Engine

Core advantage:

- scene
- objects
- placement
- logic

### 3. It Bridges 3D and 2D

Strong positioning:

- design through structure
- not only through prompts

## Main Risk

If the product is framed as:

- `AI images with layout`

it sounds like a feature, not a product.

Conclusion:

- the use case must be narrow
- the buyer must be specific
- the economic value must be explicit

## 3 Strong Directions for the Control-Layer Thesis

### Direction 1: Marketing / Creative Production

#### Who

- marketers
- performance teams

#### Pain

- they need many creatives quickly
- AI outputs are hard to control
- composition is difficult to manage

#### Solution

`Define the scene -> generate creative variations`

Examples of controlled elements:

- product on the left
- text in the safe zone
- character in the right area

#### Why It Could Work

- composition matters
- variation volume matters
- speed matters

### Direction 2: E-Commerce Visuals

#### Who

- brands
- e-commerce teams
- marketplaces

#### Pain

- product images are expensive
- photoshoots are slow and costly

#### Solution

`Build a scene -> generate product visuals`

Control includes:

- product
- background
- environment
- consistency across outputs

### Direction 3: Storyboarding / Scene Building

#### Who

- creators
- agencies

#### Pain

- scenes are hard to assemble quickly
- iteration volume is high

#### Solution

`Arrange the scene -> generate the final visual`

## Where This Will Likely Fail

- general-purpose image generation
- "just another AI image tool"
- a product without a concrete workflow and a concrete buyer

Reason:

- this is where Midjourney / Stability / OpenAI are strongest
- without a real control layer there is little differentiation

## Positioning

Not:

- `AI image generator`

But:

- `layout-first content generation`
- `control layer for generative AI`

## How to Test This Hypothesis

### Steps

1. Choose one narrow use case, for example marketing
2. Build a landing page with a simple message:
   `Create ads by placing elements, not prompting`
3. Build a fake demo:
   - one simple scene
   - 3 generation variants
4. Check whether the value is understood within 10 seconds

### Success Criteria

Not:

- "this is cool"

But:

- "this saves me time"
- "this saves me money"
- "this gives me control I do not have today"

### Key Question

Who pays:

- bad answer: "everyone"
- good answer: `performance marketers`, `e-commerce teams`, or another very specific buyer

## 5 Types of Control Layer

This is the map of the idea space.

### 1. Spatial

- where things are
- object position
- distance
- scene layout

### 2. Structural

- what the system is made of
- components
- hierarchy

### 3. Temporal

- what happens over time
- sequence
- scenarios
- transitions

### 4. Semantic

- roles
- categories
- functions

### 5. Constraint-Based

- rules
- brand guidelines
- physics
- logic

### Important Conclusion

The strongest products combine 2-3 layers at once.

## Where Failure Is Most Likely

- plain image generation
- no control layer
- no repeatable workflow
- no narrow buyer persona

## Main Strategic Insights

- do not ask where to use the engine; ask where it is required
- the strongest wedge is where `environment + action + consequence` are core
- in generative products, the value is not "generation" but "control before generation"
- positioning should be built around workflow and business value, not around the technology itself
- a narrow use case matters more than universality at the start
