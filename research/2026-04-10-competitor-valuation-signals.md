# Competitor Valuation Signals

This note is a first public pass on market-value signals for all currently tracked competitors.

The goal is not to pretend we have perfect valuation coverage. The goal is to capture the best publicly visible signals available right now and clearly separate:

- public company market caps
- private funding rounds
- disclosed or tracker-based valuations
- acquisition signals
- cases where no reliable public valuation signal was found

`Confidence` is a practical label:

- `High`: public market cap or strong mainstream reporting
- `Medium`: credible startup database / multiple secondary sources, but not official company disclosure
- `Low`: ambiguous entity match, weak tracker signal, or no clean public valuation

## Strongest Public Valuation Signals

- `Meta` and `Adobe` are public-company anchors with easy market-cap references.
- `Runway` is the clearest startup-style valuation signal in the landscape right now, at a reported `$5.3B` in February 2026.
- `Canva` is the largest private-company benchmark, but current public valuation references are inconsistent across trackers and media coverage.
- `Krea`, `Recraft`, and `Soona` have usable private-market signals from secondary databases, though not official audited disclosures.

## Practical Takeaway

The landscape splits into three valuation-transparency tiers:

1. `Public or headline-level clear`: Adobe, Meta, Runway, Canva
2. `Private but traceable through funding databases`: Krea, Recraft, Soona, ComfyUI, Ideogram, OpenArt, Vectary, Artlabs, Simplified, Freepik
3. `Mostly opaque`: a long tail of workflow players and niche vertical tools

That opacity is itself useful. It usually means one of three things:

- the company is still small
- it has not raised much institutional capital
- or it has not become important enough for the private-market data ecosystem to track cleanly yet
