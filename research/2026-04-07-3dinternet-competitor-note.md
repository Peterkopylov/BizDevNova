# 3DInternet Competitor Note

## Summary

3DInternet is an interactive training simulation and visualization company focused on utilities, energy, oil and gas, manufacturing, unions, military, government, and infrastructure-heavy industries.

It is highly relevant to the `interactive / simulation / configurator` category because it does not sell images or 3D visuals as standalone creative assets. It sells:

- simulation-based training
- safety and readiness improvement
- infrastructure visualization
- eLearning deployment
- operational risk reduction

## Why It Matters

3DInternet matters because it is one of the clearest examples in the current research where visualization and interactivity are monetized as:

- training infrastructure

not:

- content production

Its value proposition is built around:

- learning retention
- safety
- realistic practice
- reduced equipment damage
- reduced travel and off-job training time

This is a very different monetization logic from the marketing-content market.

## Key Selling Point

The clearest selling point is:

- `interactive training simulations that improve readiness, reduce risk, and save lives and equipment`

A second strong wedge is:

- `highly realistic simulation of dangerous or complex operational procedures`

This is powerful because it ties the visual / interactive layer directly to:

- safety outcomes
- employee performance
- cost reduction
- infrastructure protection

## Product Angle

3DInternet packages several layers of solution:

- interactive training simulators
- cinema-grade eLearning development
- LMS hosting
- app development
- visualization videos for accidents and infrastructure projects
- apprenticeship tracking software
- union management apps

The simulation layer itself includes:

- equipment simulation
- safety and hazard identification
- troubleshooting workflows
- first responder immersion
- control room training
- AR / VR modules

This makes 3DInternet closer to:

- an industrial simulation and training systems provider

than:

- a generic 3D tool vendor

## GTM Insight

3DInternet's GTM appears to combine:

1. industry-specific simulation solutions
2. high-trust enterprise and institutional sales
3. custom project work
4. repeatable training products
5. hosting and deployment support

This is a strong solution-sales business around mission-critical workflows.

## Implications for Us

- 3DInternet validates that interactive environments can be monetized through training and risk reduction
- one of the strongest wedges in this space is not content creation but operational consequence
- this category is very relevant to the `environment + action + consequence` thesis
- enterprise buyers here likely pay for realism, procedural fidelity, and deployment reliability rather than for flashy visuals
