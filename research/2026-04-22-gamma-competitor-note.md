# Gamma Competitor Note

Category: `AI Presentation Makers`

Suggested adjacent label: `AI Visual Storytelling Platforms`

## Summary

Gamma is one of the clearest core competitors in `AI Presentation Makers`, but it is no longer positioning itself as just a deck tool. As of April 22, 2026, Gamma frames itself as an `AI design partner` for presentations, websites, documents, social posts, graphics, and API-driven content creation.

Inside the presentation category, Gamma's strongest wedge is:

- fast AI-generated presentations
- visually polished, card-based storytelling
- easy restyling and sharing
- export to `PPT`, `PDF`, `PNG`, and `Google Slides`

Relative to Prezi, Gamma is more AI-native and creation-speed oriented. Relative to Genspark, Gamma is less research-agentic and more focused on visual communication and multi-format content generation.

Sources: [homepage](https://gamma.app/), [AI presentation maker](https://gamma.app/ai-presentation-maker), [presentations product page](https://gamma.app/products/presentations)

## Why It Matters

Gamma matters because it is a strong example of a category leader that started with AI presentations and then expanded outward into a broader visual content platform.

Its strategic importance is that it shows a credible path from:

- `AI presentation maker`

to:

- `AI design partner for business communication`

That means Gamma is relevant both as a direct deck competitor and as a sign that presentation products can stretch into websites, documents, graphics, and API workflows without abandoning the presentation wedge.

## Pricing Signals

### Public Pricing

Public pricing structure clearly visible:

- `Free`
- `Plus`
- `Pro`
- `Ultra`
- `Teams`
- `Business`

What I could verify publicly from current first-party pages:

- `Free` includes up to `10 cards` per prompt, simple presentations/docs/websites/social/images, import from `PDF & PPTX`, and export to `PDF`, `PPTX`, `PNG`, and `Google Slides`
- `Plus` includes up to `20 cards` per prompt, `1,000 monthly credits`, removes Gamma branding, advanced image models, and up to `100,000` input tokens for initial generation
- `Pro` includes up to `60 cards` per prompt on the pricing page, `4,000 monthly credits` on the help center, API access, custom branding and fonts, detailed analytics, advanced sharing, publish up to `10` custom domains, and workspace templates
- `Ultra` includes up to `75 cards` per prompt, `20,000 monthly credits`, access to the most advanced AI models across text, image, and video, publish up to `100` custom domains, and early access to new features
- `Teams` and `Business` are also offered for larger organizations

Important pricing limitation:

- the current public pricing page I reviewed exposes plan structure and included features clearly, but not cleanly searchable dollar amounts in the text snapshot
- Gamma's help center says annual plans are discounted by `28%`
- Ultra is currently monthly-only and described as being at an `introductory price`

Minor consistency note:

- the pricing page says `Pro` supports up to `60 cards` per prompt
- the help-center subscription article says `50 cards`
- I would treat that as a public documentation inconsistency worth monitoring

Sources: [pricing](https://gamma.app/pricing), [subscription help article](https://help.gamma.app/en/articles/8077107-upgrading-your-gamma-subscription), [one-month subscription help article](https://help.gamma.app/en/articles/11048064-is-there-a-one-month-subscription-option-available)

## GTM Insight

Gamma's GTM appears to work because it combines:

- instant visible value
- strong visual polish
- broad output flexibility
- a product story users can explain quickly

The company is effectively selling:

- `don't start from a blank slide`
- `make beautiful communication fast`

That is a strong wedge because presentations are high-urgency, high-visibility artifacts. Gamma then expands from that wedge into adjacent formats where the same design-and-communication engine can be reused.

## Implications for Us

- Gamma should be treated as a core benchmark competitor in `AI Presentation Makers`
- it is especially important if we care about speed, polish, and multi-format communication
- it is less directly threatening than research-agentic competitors if the category shifts toward `AI does the whole business task`
- its product expansion suggests that presentation makers can become broader `AI communication platforms`
- if we compare Gamma, Genspark, and Prezi together, Gamma currently sits in the middle: more AI-native than Prezi, less workflow-agentic than Genspark
