# Spatial Competitor Note

## Summary

`Spatial` is a 3D interactive experience and UGC platform for creators, brands, education, and enterprise training. It lets users create and publish multiplayer-ready 3D spaces and experiences across Web, Mobile, and VR.

Category fit:

- primary: `Competitor`
- sub-bucket: `multiplayer 3D experience / UGC virtual worlds / web-based immersive spaces`
- adjacent relevance: `education`, `corporate training`, `branded interactive campaigns`, `creator tooling`

## Why It Matters

Spatial is relevant because it overlaps with several important directions:

- browser-accessible multiplayer 3D experiences
- no-code and Unity-based 3D creation
- education and training use cases
- branded immersive experiences
- creator/community publishing
- AI characters / AI NPCs inside virtual environments

It is not just a 3D editor. It positions itself as an interactive media platform where spaces can be created, hosted, shared, embedded, monetized, and used by brands or institutions.

## Key Selling Point

Spatial's core promise is easy creation and publishing of interactive virtual worlds across Web, Mobile, and VR.

The strongest external message:

- create immersive multiplayer spaces without heavy setup
- use no-code templates for fast creation
- use Unity SDK for deeper customization
- publish once across web, mobile, and XR

## ICP

Likely ICPs:

- brands and agencies creating interactive marketing campaigns
- enterprises building training simulations
- educational institutions creating immersive classrooms
- creators building games, fan worlds, galleries, and social spaces
- Unity developers looking for multiplayer-ready publishing infrastructure

## Pricing Signals

Public pricing page lists:

- `Free`: `$0/month`, up to 5 concurrent users across experiences, 500MB storage per space, public links
- `Personal`: `$20/month`, non-commercial use only, unlimited spaces, 1GB storage per space, up to 50 concurrent users, Discord community access
- `Education`: custom per account/month
- `Enterprise`: custom per account/month

Enterprise and Education tiers include higher limits, advanced templates, analytics, security, SSO, external API integrations, support, and white-label / embedding capabilities.

Support docs also state that, starting August 5, 2025, commercial organizations must migrate to Enterprise to publish client-facing work or private corporate experiences unless they meet small-creator criteria.

## Official Site Updates / News Signal

Official update signal reviewed:

- Spatial Enterprise support article updated `August 5, 2025`

Key update:

- Spatial expanded Enterprise offering for brands, agencies, education, government, and organizations needing white-label publishing, SSO, analytics, or direct support
- commercial organizations now need Enterprise licensing for client-facing work or private corporate experiences unless they meet small-creator criteria

This suggests a move toward enterprise monetization, partner services, and commercial control rather than purely open creator growth.

## Control / Consistency Relevance

Spatial is relevant to control and consistency because it gives creators and brands tools to build repeatable interactive environments across devices.

Key comparison points:

- strong cross-platform publishing story
- Unity SDK gives advanced developers control
- no-code templates reduce friction but may limit deeper interaction
- brand / enterprise tiers emphasize white-label, secure hosting, analytics, and embedded distribution

Potential gap:

- creation may still depend heavily on Unity for complex interactivity, which can create a higher skill barrier than a fully native browser-first editor.

## GTM Insight

Spatial appears to use a multi-segment GTM:

- creator growth through free/personal tools and community
- enterprise monetization through brands, agencies, education, and training
- partner/studio ecosystem through referral and enterprise services network
- visible branded experiences as proof points

The interesting move is that Spatial can use creators and studios to produce commercial experiences, while enterprise clients pay for scale, security, white-labeling, and support.

## Implications for Us

- Spatial validates demand for web-accessible multiplayer 3D spaces.
- It also validates education, training, brand experiences, and embeddable 3D as commercial wedges.
- Their Unity-based toolkit is strong for developers but may leave room for simpler native creation workflows.
- Their enterprise shift suggests that hosting, analytics, white-labeling, and services around 3D experiences may be monetizable.
- Their creator/studio ecosystem supports the insight that ecosystems can help distribute and implement complex creation platforms.

## Open Questions

- How strong is Spatial's actual creation workflow for non-technical users versus Unity developers?
- How much revenue comes from Enterprise versus creators?
- Are brands buying software access, full-service campaign production, or both?
- How active is the creator economy after the enterprise licensing shift?
- Can a simpler, more accessible editor beat a Unity-based workflow for education or SMB use cases?
