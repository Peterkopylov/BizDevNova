# Manus Competitor Note

Category: `AI Presentation Makers`

Suggested adjacent label: `Agentic Research-to-Presentation Platforms`

## Summary

Manus belongs in `AI Presentation Makers`, but as a broader agent platform rather than a presentation-native specialist. As of April 22, 2026, Manus publicly offers `AI slides` that can research a topic, develop the narrative, design slides, generate visuals, and write speaker notes, with export to `PPTX`, `PDF`, and browser-based web slides.

Its strongest wedge is:

- research-to-deck automation
- agentic task execution beyond slide generation
- editable PowerPoint export
- template import and branding support

Compared with Gamma or Prezi, Manus is less presentation-native and more agentic. Compared with Prezent, it is much less brand-governance-centered. Compared with Genspark, it feels closer in spirit because both use research and task execution, but Manus positions slides as one output inside a broader general AI agent platform.

Sources: [Manus Slides docs](https://manus.im/docs/features/slides), [About](https://manus.im/about), [Home](https://www.manus.im/home)

## Why It Matters

Manus matters because it shows how the `AI Presentation Makers` category can be pulled into a more general `AI agent` workflow.

Its presentation value proposition is not just:

- make slides from a prompt

It is:

- research the topic
- structure the story
- generate visuals
- prepare speaking support
- deliver editable outputs

That makes it especially relevant if the category evolves from `AI slide maker` toward `AI agent that finishes business communication work`.

## Pricing Signals

### Public Pricing

Manus uses a credit-based pricing model.

Current publicly documented plan signals:

- `Free`: `$0/month`
- `Pro`: starting from `$20/month`, with annual billing discount of `17%`
- higher `Pro` tier: starting from `$40/month` with `7-day free trial`, also with `17%` annual billing discount
- `Team`: starting from `$20 per seat/month`, with `17%` annual billing discount

### Included Signals

`Free` publicly includes:

- Chat Mode
- Manus `1.6 Lite` in Agent Mode
- `300` daily refresh credits
- `1` concurrent task
- `2` scheduled tasks

`Pro` publicly includes:

- access to Manus `1.6 Max`, `1.6`, and `1.6 Lite`
- starting from `4,000` monthly credits on the lower Pro pricing tier
- starting from `8,000` monthly credits on the higher Pro pricing tier
- advanced research
- professional website deployment
- slide generation
- Wide Research
- `20` concurrent tasks
- `20` scheduled tasks
- beta feature early access

`Team` adds:

- all Pro features
- SSO
- data training opt-out
- team usage analytics
- internal access control
- shared slide templates

## Control / Consistency Relevance

Manus has meaningful control and consistency relevance for presentations:

- upload your own `PPTX` template
- applies layouts, colors, and fonts from that template
- supports shared slide templates on Team plans
- produces editable PowerPoint files
- can create charts from uploaded structured data

This makes it more relevant to serious business deck workflows than a simple one-shot deck generator.

At the same time, its public control story appears more `template + agent execution` than:

- deep enterprise brand governance
- strict communication compliance
- presentation-specific admin tooling

## GTM Insight

Manus's GTM appears to be:

- lead with the general AI agent story
- show presentations as one of several high-value business outputs

That is strategically important because it means Manus may win presentation use cases without competing as a pure presentation vendor. If a user already trusts Manus for:

- research
- app building
- workflow execution

then slides become one more output inside the same agent relationship.

## Implications for Us

- Manus should be tracked inside `AI Presentation Makers`, but as an `agentic research-to-presentation` sub-bucket player
- it is especially relevant where research depth, chart generation, template import, and end-to-end automation matter
- it is less presentation-native than Gamma or Prezi
- it is closer to Genspark in strategic direction, though still broader and more agent-platform-centric
- its weak review and billing signals are important risks to track
