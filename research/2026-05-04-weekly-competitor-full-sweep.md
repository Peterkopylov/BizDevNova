# Weekly Competitor Full Sweep

## Summary

The strongest materially new changes versus the latest local known state after the `2026-04-29` daily scan are concentrated in `Adobe Firefly`, `Higgsfield`, and `Runway`.

- `Adobe Firefly` now shows a clearer pricing and packaging reset on the live plans page: a new `Firefly Pro Plus` tier, first-year discounting through `2026-05-20`, and limited-time unlimited access to select partner models on higher tiers.
- `Higgsfield` is now visibly foregrounding `MCP` and `AI-agent` distribution on both its homepage and a dedicated `/mcp` page, which is a real positioning shift from `creative app with cinematic controls` toward `creative infrastructure for agents`.
- `Runway`'s live pricing page now more explicitly packages third-party model access and `Explore Mode` unlimited generation, making its marketplace-plus-platform direction clearer than the older local note.

## Biggest Changes This Week

### Adobe Firefly

- The live [Firefly plans](https://www.adobe.com/products/firefly/plans.html) page now includes `Firefly Pro Plus`, sitting between `Pro` and `Premium`.
- Adobe is also using first-year discounting on `Pro Plus` and `Premium` through `2026-05-20`.
- The same page now foregrounds limited-time unlimited generations on select partner models and resolutions, including examples such as `Kling 2.5 Turbo`, `Nano Banana Pro`, `Veo 3.1 Fast`, and `ChatGPT Image 1.5`.

### Higgsfield

- The official homepage now prominently features `HIGGSFIELD MCP` with the message `End-to-end content creation inside any AI agent`.
- The dedicated [Higgsfield MCP page](https://higgsfield.ai/mcp) shows setup for `Claude`, `OpenClaw`, `Hermes`, and `NemoClaw`.
- This is materially different from the earlier local note because the company is now selling a distribution layer into agent surfaces, not just a cinematic creation workspace.

### Runway

- The official [Runway pricing](https://runwayml.com/pricing) page now makes third-party model aggregation much more explicit on paid tiers.
- `Standard` now publicly lists access to `Veo 3.1`, `Veo 3`, and all third-party video and image models such as `Seedance 2.0`, `Kling 3.0 Pro`, `BFL FLUX.2 [max]`, and `Seedream 5.0`.
- `Unlimited` now explicitly promises unlimited generations of all image and video generation models in `Explore Mode` at a relaxed rate.

## Competitor-by-Competitor Update Highlights

### Adobe Firefly

- The important shift is not just one more feature. Adobe is pairing the already captured late-April agentic launches with a clearer monetization ladder for heavy users.
- `Firefly Pro Plus` and the limited-time unlimited partner-model offer make Adobe look more aggressive about locking users into Firefly as the aggregation layer for multiple leading models.

### Higgsfield

- Higgsfield is now more clearly acting like an `agent-ready creative infrastructure` company.
- The homepage treatment suggests `MCP` is a lead wedge, alongside `Canvas`, `Marketing Studio`, `GPT Image 2`, and `Seedance 2.0`.

### Runway

- Runway's current surface sharpens the story that it is becoming both a first-party model company and a bundled access layer for outside models.
- The `Explore Mode` language matters because it reframes the unlimited tier around experimentation volume and workflow breadth, not only higher credit caps.

## Pricing and GTM Changes

- `Adobe Firefly` now has a clearer paid-tier ladder with `Pro Plus` and time-boxed unlimited-model promotions, which is a real pricing/package change.
- `Runway` now more explicitly bundles third-party model access inside paid subscriptions and positions `Unlimited` around `Explore Mode`.
- `Higgsfield`'s change is more GTM and positioning than list-price: the company is visibly pushing distribution through agents and automated workflows.

## Why It Matters

- The market continues to move toward `aggregation + workflow + distribution`, not only model quality.
- Adobe is using partner-model bundling and pricing design to become the safer mainstream aggregation layer.
- Higgsfield is pushing from creator tool toward agent-connected infrastructure.
- Runway is strengthening its `platform plus model marketplace` posture, which increases pressure on narrower tools that do not own either workflow depth or distribution.
