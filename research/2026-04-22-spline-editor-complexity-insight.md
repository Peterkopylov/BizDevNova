# Spline Editor Complexity Insight

## Summary

One important practical insight for the `3D website builders` category:

- `Spline` is powerful
- but the editor appears meaningfully complex for mainstream website users

That matters because category winners are not determined only by capability.

They are also determined by:

- how quickly a user can get to a satisfying result
- how much 3D knowledge is required
- whether the workflow feels like `designing a website` or `operating a 3D tool`

## Why This Matters

Spline's strength is also its friction.

It offers:

- real 3D scene building
- materials
- lighting
- animation
- states and events
- variables, APIs, and webhooks
- production embeds and exports

But that power means the user is often dealing with:

- a true scene editor
- spatial thinking
- object hierarchy
- lighting/camera decisions
- interaction logic
- optimization tradeoffs

So for many users, Spline is not:

- `easy website builder with 3D`

It is closer to:

- `accessible professional 3D tool`

## Strategic Interpretation

This is a strong market signal.

It suggests a real gap between:

- what expert or design-forward users want

and:

- what mainstream users can actually operate comfortably

That gap creates room for products that offer:

- stronger defaults
- easier prompting
- simpler layout control
- prebuilt 3D interaction patterns
- website-first workflows instead of scene-first workflows

## Category Implication

This is one reason `Dora`, `Omma`, or future products in this space may matter even if Spline is technically stronger in some layers.

If Spline feels too complex, then a simpler product can win by offering:

- less freedom
- but much faster time-to-value

In other words:

- `Spline` may win on depth and control
- but lighter tools may win on adoption, speed, and approachability

## Product Insight

There may be two separate product opportunities in this category:

### 1. `Power-user 3D web creation`

Best for:

- agencies
- creative developers
- advanced designers
- brand teams with strong visual craft

### 2. `3D website creation for mainstream users`

Best for:

- freelancers
- small businesses
- marketers
- founders
- people who want immersive output without learning a 3D tool

If this split is real, then `editor complexity` is not a minor UX issue.

It is a category-shaping variable.

## Practical Takeaway

The clearest takeaway is:

- `Spline` validates that the market wants high-quality interactive 3D
- but its editor complexity may prevent it from becoming the default answer for broader website creation

That means the biggest opportunity may be:

- keeping enough of Spline's visual power
- while dramatically reducing the operational burden on the user
