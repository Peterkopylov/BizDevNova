# Social Media Guideline: Comment First, Post Second

## Summary

One practical view of what is working in 2026:

- the old `post 3x a day and pray` approach is no longer enough

Instead, a stronger operating model includes:

- genuine engagement before posting
- short-form video that teaches one clear thing
- social SEO
- save-worthy content
- aggressive repurposing

## Core Idea

Posting should not happen in isolation.

A stronger workflow is to warm up the account and audience context before publishing, then publish content that is highly searchable, useful, and reusable across formats.

## 1. Comment First, Post Second

One tactic that appears to work well:

- spend around 15 minutes doing genuine engagement before posting

This can include:

- replying to people
- leaving thoughtful comments
- engaging with relevant posts in the niche

### Why It Matters

This can help generate:

- profile visits
- relationship momentum
- audience awareness before the post goes live

The claim here is notable:

- sometimes the pre-post commenting drives more profile visits than the content itself

## 2. Short-Form Video That Teaches One Thing

The recommendation is not to post vague lifestyle or `day in the life` filler.

A stronger format is:

- answer one specific question
- in under 60 seconds

### Why It Works

This format is:

- focused
- easier to understand
- more useful
- more likely to hold attention

## 3. Social SEO

The idea here is that users increasingly search TikTok and Instagram the way they search Google.

That means discoverability can improve when keywords appear in:

- captions
- hashtags
- subtitles

### Practical Takeaway

Content should not only be optimized for feed distribution.

It should also be optimized for search-based discovery inside social platforms.

## 4. Save-Worthy Content

Another key point:

- saves can matter more than likes

Content is more likely to earn saves when it gives people something they want to keep and reuse later, such as:

- checklists
- templates
- step-by-step instructions

### Why It Matters

Save-worthy content often signals:

- usefulness
- future intent
- repeat value

## 5. Repurpose Everything

A strong content operations principle:

- one piece of source content should become multiple assets

Example:

- one blog post
- one Twitter thread
- one LinkedIn carousel
- two short-form videos

### Why It Matters

This improves:

- efficiency
- reach across channels
- consistency of publishing

## Working System

This note suggests a practical content stack:

1. engage before posting
2. publish one focused, useful asset
3. optimize it for search and saves
4. repurpose it across formats and platforms

## Good Reminder

The goal is not just volume.

The goal is useful content, better distribution, and more leverage from each idea.
