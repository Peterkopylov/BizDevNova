# Product Ideas Batch

## Summary

This note collects multiple early product ideas that are not yet developed enough to justify one file per idea.

They are grouped here for later filtering, expansion, and prioritization.

## Social and Community Ideas

### Human or Not

Game concept where players try to distinguish real humans from AI.

Angle:

- reproduce or reinterpret the core mechanic
- focus on social deception, speed, and replayability

### Event Planning with Human Behavior

Product idea around planning events while also modeling how people may behave inside them.

Angle:

- not just logistics
- also social dynamics, movement, friction points, or audience behavior

### AI Agentic Workflows / Virtual Office

Product direction where agentic workflows run like a lightweight virtual office.

Potential framing:

- persistent agents
- low-cost always-on operation
- very cheap pricing logic, such as `cent per day`

## Creative and Consumer Experience Ideas

### Manga Creation as a Standalone Product

Separate product focused specifically on manga creation rather than generic creative tooling.

Potential wedge:

- manga-native templates
- panel workflows
- character consistency
- speech and story layout support

### Lego from Tiny Balls

3D building experience where users assemble objects from small sticky balls.

Why it feels interesting:

- tactile
- satisfying sound feedback
- semi-meditative creative play
- like a 3D mosaic or soft construction system

### TikTok of Games

Instead of scrolling short videos, users scroll through tiny playable games lasting around `20 seconds`.

Potential value:

- stronger interaction than passive video
- fast novelty loop
- extremely low-commitment entertainment

### 3D Puzzles / Complex Assemblies

Consumer or hobby product where users assemble complex 3D structures themselves, such as engines, airplanes, or mechanical systems.

Core appeal:

- the fun is in building it yourself
- digital equivalent of hands-on model assembly

### Parkour Games

Simple direction worth exploring as a game/use-case cluster.

Open angle:

- can the existing engine and creation stack make parkour-style content especially easy or fun to build?

## Niche and Vertical Ideas

### Service for Wealthy Customers at Small Volumes

Business idea around building something low-volume but high-value for wealthier customers.

This is intentionally broad for now and needs narrowing by:

- customer type
- service type
- value density
- required human touch

### Fire Brigade Training

Narrow training market using 3D material and simulations for fire brigade education.

Why it matters:

- concrete market
- training use case
- possible network entry through existing contacts

### Configurator Builder for SMB

Builder product for small and medium businesses that need configurators without enterprise-level complexity.

### Configurators for Newly Introduced Products

Focus on products that have only recently appeared on the market and therefore do not yet have mature configurator workflows around them.

Potential angle:

- speed matters more than deep legacy integration
- useful for categories where configurators exist eventually, but not immediately

## 3D Media and Interaction Ideas

### Fast Creation of Stereopairs

Tool for quickly creating stereopairs that can be viewed by people who know how to see them without special hardware.

### 3D Video Generation with Red-Green / Anaglyph Angle

Product or feature direction around stereoscopic / anaglyph-style 3D video generation.

### Recreate the Camera Operator Process

Video feature idea based on reproducing the operator's job of switching between cameras.

Potential framing:

- multi-camera logic
- automated live direction
- cinematic video support

## Simulation and Education Ideas

### Orchestrator for Real-Life Situations

Simulation product that helps users explore what may happen in a specific human situation.

Example framing:

- what happens if I approach these two people?
- how might the interaction develop?

This overlaps with relationship and social-dynamics simulation, but with a stronger orchestration layer.

### AI-Generated Ready-Made Courses

Tool or platform for generating complete courses with AI.

Example:

- language learning course as a series of dialogues with NPCs

Potential advantage:

- can be generated at scale
- works especially well for scenario-based learning

## Loose Technical / Product Prompts

### Use of Sound

Open product prompt:

- how can sound be used more meaningfully inside the product or experience?
