# Virti Competitor Note

Category: `AI conversation simulation`

Suggested adjacent label: `AI Roleplay and Immersive Learning Platform`

## Summary

Virti is a strong direct competitor in `AI conversation simulation`, especially where simulated conversations are part of a broader immersive-learning stack. As of April 22, 2026, Virti positions itself as an `AI role-play and video training platform` that combines virtual humans, interactive video, analytics, and multi-format learning delivery.

Its clearest wedge is not generic AI chat. It is:

- AI roleplay scenarios
- immersive video training
- virtual humans
- workplace learning and confidence-building
- analytics-driven training improvement

Compared with narrower AI roleplay products, Virti appears broader and more platform-like, spanning soft skills, medical training, customer service, leadership, and sales.

## Pricing Signals

### Public Pricing

Virti has a public pricing page, but the surfaced content does not show clear public plan prices in the accessible snapshot.

### Included Signals

The clearest verified pricing and packaging signals are:

- `14-day free trial`
- pricing uses a `credit` model for Virtual Human training
- credits are consumed based on modality and time spent training
- unused credits do not roll over
- plans can be changed or canceled from the billing page
- customers can start small and scale

Verified credit mechanics:

- `1 credit = 20 minutes` of `3D rendered Virtual Human` training
- `1 credit = 5 minutes` of `video avatar` training
- `1 credit = 2.5 minutes` of `advanced voice` training

This is more detailed publicly than many competitors, even without exposed plan prices.

## GTM Insight

Virti's GTM looks broader than a pure sales-roleplay competitor.

Its stack appears to include:

- free trial entry
- enterprise platform sale
- broad cross-industry positioning
- strong healthcare and education credibility
- immersive learning differentiation
- roleplay plus video plus analytics bundle

The strongest GTM lesson is:

- AI conversation simulation can become more defensible when bundled into a larger experiential-learning platform instead of sold as a standalone conversation tool

## Implications for Us

Virti is a strong direct competitor if our focus is:

- enterprise AI roleplay training
- scenario-based learning
- multi-format immersive training
- soft-skills and safety/confidence development

The strongest takeaways are:

- the category extends well beyond sales into healthcare, customer service, education, and leadership
- no-code scenario creation matters
- video and immersive media can strengthen the value proposition around conversation simulation
- analytics and reporting are central to making this budget-worthy
