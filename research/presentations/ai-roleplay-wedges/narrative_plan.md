# AI Roleplay Wedges Deck

Audience:

- internal product and strategy team

Objective:

- summarize the market with a more execution-oriented framing
- show the key competitors and the public scale/funding signals we can verify
- explain why the category is really about solving business problems, not generic dialogue
- clarify our wedge around multi-NPC and spatial interaction
- define the minimum credible MVP

Narrative arc:

1. The category is growing, but there is no clean standalone "AI roleplay market" number
2. Competitors already validate the demand for training, readiness, and rehearsal
3. The market is crowded at the generic roleplay layer, but not at the orchestration and spatial layer
4. Multi-NPC and spatial are not just features; together they create a sharper product wedge
5. We still need the table-stakes workflow: prompt -> scenario -> play mode -> assessment

Slide list:

1. Market size and CAGR
2. Key competitors table
3. The market buys business-problem solving, not dialogue
4. Core features every credible competitor has
5. Our potential wedges: multi-NPC and spatial
6. Multi-NPC: who has it, what it solves, why orchestration matters
7. Spatial: who has it, what it solves, what it unlocks
8. Spatial + multi-NPC as the wedge, plus adjacent wedges
9. Necessary features, strengthening features, and realtime exploration
10. MVP shape

Visual system:

- warm editorial background
- dark graphite type
- blue-violet primary accent
- teal secondary accent
- coral tertiary accent
- card-based layouts with enough whitespace to stay readable in a room

Source plan:

- workspace research notes created in this project
- current public market estimates for conversational AI and AI in education/training adjacencies
- public funding news where we have already verified it
