# Social Media Guideline: Reply in the First Hour

## Summary

One simple but effective social media habit:

- reply to every comment in the first hour after posting

It may seem small, but it can help kickstart engagement and increase reach.

## Core Idea

The first hour after posting is often one of the most important windows for traction.

If people comment and the conversation keeps going, that creates stronger early interaction signals around the post.

## Why It Can Work

Replying quickly can help:

- keep the conversation active
- encourage more back-and-forth
- make commenters more likely to return
- signal that the post is generating interaction

In practice, this can improve:

- reach
- engagement depth
- visibility of the post

## Observed Result

The reported experience was:

- posts started getting noticeably more reach after building the habit of replying to every single comment in the first hour

Even though the tactic seems small, the cumulative effect was meaningful.

## Practical Lesson

Posting is not the full job.

The immediate engagement window after posting matters too.

A post should be treated more like the start of a conversation than the end of a publishing task.

## Repeatable Workflow

### Before Posting

- leave time in your schedule for the first hour
- avoid posting right before meetings or deep work blocks

### In the First Hour

- reply to every comment
- keep the conversation going
- ask short follow-up questions where natural
- reward thoughtful comments with thoughtful replies

### Afterward

- continue replying later as needed
- track whether posts with active first-hour engagement perform better over time

## Best Use Cases

This is especially useful for:

- founder-led social accounts
- brand accounts trying to build community
- posts designed to trigger comments
- early-stage accounts where every engagement signal matters

## Good Reminder

Early interaction is not only about vanity metrics.

It can shape distribution, conversation quality, and the long-tail performance of a post.
