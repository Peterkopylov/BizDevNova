# Weekly Competitor Full Sweep

## Summary

Materially new changes versus the latest local known state from the `2026-05-21` daily scan and the `2026-05-18` weekly sweep were found in `Runway` and `FLORA`.

- `Runway` launched `Aleph 2.0` and `Edit Studio` on `2026-05-21`, adding a clearer `edit existing footage` wedge on top of its broader generation platform.
- `FLORA` launched official `MCP`, `API`, and `CLI` surfaces on `2026-05-19`, extending the product from a collaborative canvas into callable creative infrastructure.

No stronger new funding, valuation, acquisition, or market-structure signal was verified for the tracked set in this pass.

## Biggest Changes This Week

### Runway

- The official [`Introducing Aleph 2.0 and Edit Studio`](https://runwayml.com/news/introducing-aleph-2-and-edit-studio) post on `2026-05-21` is a material product and GTM change versus the latest local baseline.
- `Aleph 2.0` upgrades Runway's flagship video editing model and `Edit Studio` wraps it in a product flow designed around previewing edits before generating final video.
- Runway is now selling a more explicit ROI story around making more campaign, seasonal, and post-production variants from footage teams already have, not only generating net-new content.

### FLORA

- The official [`FLORA, everywhere, now via MCP, API, and CLI`](https://flora.ai/blog/introducing-the-flora-mcp-api-cli) post on `2026-05-19` is a material distribution and product-surface expansion that was not captured in the latest local sweep artifacts.
- FLORA is now directly callable from `Claude`, `ChatGPT`, `Cursor`, and other MCP-compatible clients, plus automation pipelines and terminals through its `API` and `CLI`.
- This changes the read on FLORA from `canvas-centric workflow layer` to `workflow layer plus creative infrastructure`, which is a stronger strategic posture.

## Competitor-by-Competitor Update Highlights

### Runway

- The important new change is not a new base model family. It is the stronger packaging of `AI-assisted post-production` as a core subscription use case.
- The combination of `30s` `1080p` editing, image-level preview control, targeted preservation of the rest of the video, and multi-shot edits makes Runway more relevant to teams trying to multiply existing footage instead of always generating from scratch.

### FLORA

- The important new change is not another canvas feature. It is official workflow reach into the environments where teams already think and ship.
- The post's examples are commercially specific: growth teams generating hook variants in chat, DTC teams calling techniques at scale through the API, and agencies replacing in-house AI tooling with FLORA-backed workflows.

## Why It Matters

- The workflow-layer competitors are still moving away from single-surface products and toward more embedded operating layers.
- Runway's newest move suggests the next competitive wedge in video is not only generation quality, but cost-effective post-production and campaign iteration from existing source assets.
- FLORA's new surfaces strengthen the thesis that the control layer may win not just by better UX, but by being reachable wherever teams already work.
