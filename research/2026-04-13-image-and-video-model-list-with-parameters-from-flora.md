## Quick Product Implications

- Based on this FLORA-sourced snapshot, FLORA is clearly pricing itself as a `model router + workflow environment`, not as a single-model product.
- The spread between cheap and expensive models is large enough that workflow UX and cost visibility are probably important parts of the product value.
- High-cost items like `Lora Trainer`, `Marey`, `Kling O3 Pro`, `xAI Grok Imagine`, and `Google Veo 3.1` suggest FLORA is exposing frontier model economics directly rather than hiding them completely.
- Very cheap models like `Z Image Turbo`, `FLUX 2`, `Gemini 2.0 Flash`, and `Tencent LTXV` make it easier for FLORA to support fast iteration before users switch into more expensive premium generations.
- This pricing structure supports FLORA's positioning as a `creative operating system` more than as a fixed-output SaaS.
