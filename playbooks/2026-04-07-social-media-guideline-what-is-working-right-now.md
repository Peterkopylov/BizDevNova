# Social Media Guideline: What Is Working Right Now

## Summary

One current view of what is working across social platforms:

- short-form video still dominates
- raw and real often beats over-produced
- teaching or problem-solving content outperforms generic promotion
- niche focus beats broad targeting
- consistency beats chasing isolated viral hits
- growth also comes from comments and DMs, not only posting
- AI can improve speed, but human-sounding content still wins

## Core Idea

What works right now is not especially complicated.

The strongest content tends to be:

- easy to consume
- useful
- specific
- human
- consistent

## Short-Form Video

Short-form video continues to perform strongly across platforms, especially when it feels:

- raw
- direct
- real

That often works better than content that feels too polished or over-produced.

## Useful Content Beats Generic Promotion

Content that teaches something or helps solve a specific problem tends to perform better than generic promotional posts.

That means stronger content often does one of the following:

- explains something clearly
- shows how to do something
- helps avoid a mistake
- addresses a narrow pain point

## Niche Focus Beats Broad Messaging

Trying to talk to everyone usually weakens the message.

Content performs better when it is aimed at:

- a clear niche
- a specific type of customer
- a recognizable problem

Specificity tends to create stronger relevance.

## Consistency Matters More Than Viral Chasing

A repeatable publishing habit is often more useful than constantly trying to create one breakout post.

This matters because:

- consistency compounds
- audience trust builds over time
- distribution improves with repeated signals

## Growth Does Not Only Come From Posting

A lot of growth also comes from active participation:

- replying in comments
- engaging in DMs
- continuing conversations after the post goes live

This reinforces the idea that social growth is not only a content output game. It is also an interaction game.

## AI Helps With Speed, But Human Tone Still Wins

AI can be useful for:

- faster drafting
- idea generation
- repurposing
- content operations

But the winning content still tends to feel:

- human
- relatable
- natural

If the content sounds artificial or generic, the speed advantage does not help much.

## Working Heuristic

If choosing where to focus effort, prioritize:

- short-form video
- useful content
- clear niche targeting
- consistent publishing
- active engagement after posting
- human tone

## Good Reminder

The goal is not only to publish.

The goal is to make content that feels real, useful, and relevant to a specific audience.
