# AI Roleplays Category Terminology Note

## Question

Is `AI roleplays` a general term?

## Short Answer

Yes.

`AI roleplays` is a broad general term for products or features where AI simulates a person, stakeholder, customer, interviewer, manager, or other participant so a user can practice a conversation or scenario.

It is useful as an umbrella category label, but it is usually too broad to describe a product precisely on its own.

## What `AI Roleplays` Usually Covers

In practice, the term can include:

- sales roleplay
- interview practice
- leadership and management conversations
- customer support training
- negotiation practice
- public speaking rehearsal
- soft-skills training
- education and classroom simulations
- onboarding and certification scenarios

## Why It Is a General Term

The phrase is broad because it does not specify:

- who the user is
- what kind of conversation is being simulated
- whether the product is for individuals or teams
- whether the use case is training, coaching, rehearsal, or evaluation
- whether the simulation is text, voice, video, or avatar-based

Because of that, `AI roleplays` works well as a top-level category phrase, but not always as a sharp positioning statement.

## Better Subcategory Labels

Depending on the context, more precise labels are usually better:

### When the focus is broad simulated conversation practice

- `AI conversation simulation`

### When the focus is enterprise training

- `AI roleplay training`
- `workplace conversation simulator`
- `AI coaching simulation`

### When the focus is sales

- `AI sales roleplay`
- `AI sales conversation simulator`

### When the focus is communication improvement

- `AI communication coaching`
- `AI communication rehearsal`

### When the focus is interviews and presentations

- `AI interview practice`
- `AI presentation rehearsal`

## How to Use the Term in Research

Best practice:

- use `AI roleplays` as a broad umbrella term
- use more specific labels when describing a competitor, product wedge, or category segment

Example:

- Yoodli can be described broadly as an `AI roleplay` platform
- more precisely, it fits `AI communication coaching` and `AI conversation simulation`

- Skillsoft CAISY can be described broadly as `AI roleplay training`
- more precisely, it fits `enterprise workplace conversation simulation`

- DialogueTrainer can be described broadly as `AI roleplay` for soft skills
- more precisely, it fits `simulation-based communication training`

## Recommended Working Taxonomy

For our research, the cleanest structure is:

- top-level umbrella: `AI roleplays`
- broader analytical category: `AI conversation simulation`
- more specific wedges:
- `AI sales roleplay`
- `AI workplace conversation simulation`
- `AI communication coaching`
- `AI interview and presentation rehearsal`
- `multi-party conversation simulation`

## Recommendation

Use:

- `AI roleplays` when we want a simple, understandable umbrella label

Prefer:

- `AI conversation simulation` when doing category analysis

Use narrower labels when describing direct competitors or product positioning.
