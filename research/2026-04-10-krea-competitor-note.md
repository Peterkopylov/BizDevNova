# Krea Competitor Note

## Summary

Krea is a broad AI creative suite for images, video, 3D, realtime rendering, editing, and fine-tuning.

It matters because it sells:

- one subscription for many leading models
- fast iteration
- realtime generation
- custom fine-tuning and pro workflows

## Why It Matters

Krea matters because it is one of the strongest `all models in one place` competitors with:

- image generation
- editing
- video
- 3D
- realtime workflows
- LoRA training

It is closer to a creative operating system than to a single image generator.

## Pricing Signals

### Public Pricing

Krea publishes official pricing:

- `Free`: `$0`, `100 compute units/day`
- `Basic`: `$9/month`, `5,000 compute units/month`
- `Pro`: `$35/month`, `20,000 compute units/month`
- `Max`: `$105/month`, `60,000 compute units/month`
- `Enterprise`: custom

### Included Signals

- paid plans include commercial license
- LoRA training and premium model access are major upsell levers
- `Max` includes unlimited relaxed generations and unlimited LoRA fine-tunings at higher file limits
- team/enterprise positioning emphasizes integrations, SLAs, and higher-volume use

## Control / Consistency Relevance

Krea is highly relevant to our thesis because it combines:

- realtime control
- editing
- LoRA training
- multi-model access
- image, video, and 3D in one workspace

It is not vertically packaged, but it is strong on power-user creative control.

## GTM Insight

Krea's GTM appears to combine:

1. freemium entry
2. power-user upsell through compute and premium model access
3. broad suite positioning
4. strong enterprise/contact-sales layer on top

## Implications for Us

- Krea is one of the most relevant horizontal competitors if we move toward a broad creative suite
- it validates that users will pay for control, speed, and multi-model aggregation
- its weak external review signal suggests room for differentiated workflow UX
