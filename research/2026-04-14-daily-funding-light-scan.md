## Company-Level Signals

### Canva

Material acquisition / market-structure signal:

- Canva acquired `Simtheory`, an AI workspace for custom agents, and `Ortto`, a customer-data and marketing-automation platform.
- Canva explicitly said these deals help it evolve from `a design platform with AI tools` into `an AI platform with design and productivity tools at its core`.
- Canva also tied the deals to the full marketing lifecycle: ideate, create, edit, publish, measure, and optimize in one system.

Why it matters:

- this is not an image-model move; it is a `workflow capture` move
- it raises Canva’s threat level as an end-to-end operating layer for marketing and branded content teams
- it reinforces the market shift from asset generation toward connected creative systems

### OpenArt

Material product / positioning signal:

- OpenArt is now visibly leaning into `multi-model orchestration` instead of single-model attachment.
- It added `IP Safety Check`, which is a trust/commercialization signal rather than just a generation feature.
- It launched `Character Builder` and `Worlds`, pushing deeper into persistent character consistency and navigable 3D creation.
- Its blog and navigation now emphasize `video`, `audio`, `3D models`, `Worlds`, `AI Character`, and `AI Influencer`, not just image generation.

Why it matters:

- OpenArt is moving from `AI art app` toward `creative production environment`
- the `Worlds` direction is specifically relevant to our `2D-to-3D / 3D-to-2D / controllable scene` tracking
- the IP-safety framing makes it more credible for commercial creator and brand use cases

### Runway

No new funding event beyond the already-captured `2026-02-10` Series E and `2026-03-31` fund/program announcements.

But the official surface now makes three things clearer:

- `Runway Characters` is a real-time video agent API, not just a demo feature
- `Runway Builders` is trying to lock in early-stage startups with credits, access, and community
- `Runway Fund` formalizes Runway’s role as an ecosystem shaper around world-model applications

Why it matters:

- Runway is building a control point above the model layer
- its strategy now spans model vendor, API platform, developer program, and investor
- that makes it strategically more comparable to an infrastructure-and-distribution platform than to a standalone creative tool

## Why It Matters

The new information in this scan does **not** change the funding leaderboard.

It **does** sharpen the market map:

- `Canva` is the clearest signal that design/workflow incumbents are absorbing agentic AI and marketing automation, not just adding generators.
