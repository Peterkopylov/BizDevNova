# Social Media Guideline: Listen Before Writing

## Summary

One useful social media principle:

- stop trying to invent captions first
- start by listening to how the audience already talks

The strongest captions often come from the exact words people use when describing their own frustrations, goals, and situations.

## Core Idea

Instead of writing in generic marketing language, spend time in places where the target audience is already talking:

- comment sections
- Reddit threads
- competitor posts
- community discussions

The goal is not to sell there.

The goal is to collect real language.

## Why It Works

People usually do not respond strongly to abstract marketing phrases such as:

- `streamline your workflow`
- `boost productivity`

They respond more to language that feels familiar and emotionally accurate.

If a caption sounds like something the audience would actually say, it is more likely to:

- stop the scroll
- feel relevant
- generate comments
- create recognition

## Example

In one case, content was being created for a scheduling tool.

The team kept writing captions like:

- `streamline your workflow and boost productivity`

That language did not resonate.

But in the comments under competitor posts, people were saying:

- `I just want to stop missing meetings because of timezone confusion`

That phrase was then used in the next caption.

Result:

- engagement on that post was noticeably higher than other posts that month

## Practical Lesson

Do not start with messaging invention.

Start with audience listening.

Then convert the audience's own language into:

- hooks
- captions
- post angles
- ad copy
- landing page language

## Repeatable Workflow

### Step 1: Find Live Audience Language

Look in:

- competitor Instagram or LinkedIn comments
- Reddit threads
- product review sites
- support forums
- niche communities

### Step 2: Collect Exact Phrases

Capture language around:

- frustrations
- desired outcomes
- workarounds
- objections
- confusion

### Step 3: Look for Repeated Patterns

Pay attention to:

- repeated pain points
- emotional wording
- simple phrases people naturally use

### Step 4: Reuse the Language Carefully

Turn those phrases into:

- caption openings
- post hooks
- CTA language
- content themes

The goal is not to copy comments word for word every time.

The goal is to write in the audience's natural language instead of brand-invented language.

## Good Reminder

People do not engage with captions that sound like marketing.

They engage with captions that sound like something they would say themselves.

## Best Use Cases

This approach is especially useful for:

- social media content
- ad creative messaging
- landing page copy
- email hooks
- founder-led content
