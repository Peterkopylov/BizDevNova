# Marble Competitor Note

Category: `2d to 3d`

Suggested adjacent label: `Image-to-Editable-3D Worlds / World Models`

## Summary

Marble is currently one of the clearest productized competitors in the `2d to 3d` landscape when the target outcome is not just a 3D asset, but an `editable, persistent, explorable 3D world`. As of April 23, 2026, World Labs positions Marble as a multimodal world-creation product that can generate high-fidelity 3D worlds from:

- text
- a single image
- multiple images
- video
- 360 panoramas
- coarse 3D layouts

The strongest competitive point is not only the generation itself. It is that Marble publicly exposes a broader workflow layer around:

- editing
- world expansion
- composition
- export
- 3D-layout control

That makes Marble one of the strongest current examples of a product that turns `2D image -> editable 3D environment` into a real commercial workflow rather than a pure research demo.

Sources: [World Labs homepage](https://www.worldlabs.ai/), [Marble announcement](https://www.worldlabs.ai/blog/marble-world-model), [Marble docs overview](https://docs.worldlabs.ai/index)

## Why It Matters

Marble matters because it appears to sit at the intersection of:

- world-model research

## Pricing Signals

### Public Pricing Structure

Official first-party docs clearly confirm a four-tier subscription system:

- `Free`
- `Standard`
- `Pro`
- `Max`

The first-party billing docs also confirm feature segmentation:

- `Free`: text, single-image, or panorama world generation; up to `4` world generations
- `Standard`: multi-image, video, or 3D-layout generation; editing; exporting; community downloads; up to `12` world generations
- `Pro`: expansion workflows, better video quality, high-quality textured mesh export, commercial rights; up to `25` world generations
- `Max`: high-volume production; up to `75` world generations

### Price Points

The official billing docs do not expose the entire price table in the text snapshot I reviewed, but they do include a worked example confirming:

- `Standard`: `$20/month`
- `Pro`: `$35/month`

Third-party reporting from the Marble launch consistently reported the full monthly pricing as:

- `Free`: `$0`
- `Standard`: `$20/month`
- `Pro`: `$35/month`
- `Max`: `$95/month`

I am treating the `Standard` and `Pro` prices as strongly supported by first-party documentation, and the full four-plan price table as high-confidence but partly third-party-reported.

### Credits Signals

The billing docs also confirm:

- Marble is credit-based
- a basic single-image world generation example uses `1,580` credits
- a more advanced multi-image + edits + expand workflow example uses `3,900` credits
- subscription credits do not roll over
- top-up credits do roll over and expire after `1 year`

Sources: [Subscriptions & billing](https://docs.worldlabs.ai/marble/support/account-billing), [TechCrunch launch coverage](https://techcrunch.com/2025/11/12/fei-fei-lis-world-labs-speeds-up-the-world-model-race-with-marble-its-first-commercial-product/)

## Implications for Us

- Marble is one of the strongest direct competitors in `image -> editable 3D environment`.
- It raises the bar from `generate a world` to `generate, edit, expand, compose, and export a world`.
- Its workflow breadth may be more strategically important than raw first-generation visual quality alone.
- The combination of strong founder credibility, major funding, public docs, and actual product packaging makes it one of the most serious players to track.
- If this category matures, Marble is one of the clearest candidates to become a platform layer rather than just a novelty creation tool.

## Open Questions

- How robust is Marble’s editing fidelity after many sequential edits and expansions in real production workflows?
- How strong is the mesh export quality relative to traditional DCC or environment-building pipelines?
- Can Marble become a daily workflow tool for studios, or does it remain strongest for ideation and early environment generation?
- How much of the long-term moat comes from the model itself versus the surrounding workflow stack like Chisel, Spark, and Marble Labs?
- Will World Labs expand from creator workflows into more enterprise-specific simulation, digital-twin, or robotics use cases faster than its competitors?
