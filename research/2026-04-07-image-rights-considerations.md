# Image Rights Considerations

## Summary

Image rights should be treated as a core product and go-to-market consideration in any AI image-generation category analysis.

This is not a side issue. Rights affect:

- customer trust
- enterprise adoption
- commercial usability
- legal risk
- platform positioning

## Why This Matters

For many buyers, especially teams and companies, the question is not only:

- can this tool generate good images?

It is also:

- can we safely use those images commercially?
- who owns the output?
- what happens if the model was trained on problematic data?
- are there restrictions for branding, publishing, ads, or resale?

## Key Rights Questions To Track

When evaluating competitors or shaping our own offering, these are the main questions to document:

### 1. Commercial Usage Rights

- Can users use generated images in commercial work?
- Are commercial rights included by default or only on paid plans?
- Are there restrictions for agencies, client work, ads, packaging, or resale?

### 2. Ownership of Outputs

- Does the customer own the generated image?
- Does the platform keep any rights to reuse, display, or train on outputs?
- Are rights different for free vs paid tiers?

### 3. Training Data Risk

- Is the model trained on licensed, public, or unclear datasets?
- Does the company address copyright exposure?
- Does the company provide any indemnity or legal reassurance?

### 4. Likeness and Identity Rights

- Can users generate characters based on real people?
- What protections exist around celebrity likeness or personal identity?
- Are there policies around impersonation or misuse?

### 5. IP Infringement Risk

- Can generated outputs unintentionally resemble copyrighted characters or styles?
- Does the company warn users about this?
- Are there moderation or compliance safeguards?

### 6. Client and Enterprise Readiness

- Are terms clear enough for agencies and enterprise procurement?
- Are there separate enterprise terms?
- Is there support for indemnity, private deployments, or custom licensing?

## Why This Matters Strategically

Rights are often one of the biggest differences between:

- a fun creator tool
- and a serious production platform

For enterprise buyers, unclear rights can block adoption even if the product quality is strong.

For agencies, rights matter because they deliver work to clients and need confidence in commercial use.

For brand teams, rights risk can outweigh creative upside.

## Signals To Look For In Competitors

Useful signals when reviewing a competitor:

- explicit `commercial use included` messaging
- separate enterprise plans with custom terms
- references to legal risk, copyright, or licensing
- documentation for publishing, print, advertising, or client delivery
- statements about whether user assets or outputs are reused for training

## Implications for Us

If we explore image generation or control-layer products, rights should be part of the product definition from the start.

Questions we would eventually need clear answers to:

- What rights do users get over outputs?
- Can teams use outputs in commercial campaigns?
- Do we need different rights policies for self-serve vs enterprise?
- Is legal clarity itself part of the value proposition?
