# Shared AI Roleplay in Multiplayer

## Summary

Product idea: let multiple users experience AI-driven roleplay together inside a shared multiplayer environment.

## Core Concept

Instead of a one-person chat with an AI character, the product creates a multiplayer space where several users participate in the same AI-mediated roleplay session.

The AI can act as:

- a character
- a game master
- a world simulator
- a facilitator of social interaction

This turns AI roleplay into a shared experience rather than a private prompt flow.

## Why It Could Matter

- multiplayer makes AI roleplay more social, memorable, and replayable
- shared roleplay can create stronger emotional engagement than solo interaction
- users can co-create stories, decisions, and outcomes together
- this can sit between gaming, entertainment, social products, and creative tools

## Potential Use Cases

- collaborative story worlds
- social deduction or mystery scenarios
- AI-led improv experiences
- relationship or communication simulations done with multiple participants
- educational historical or training roleplay
- branded interactive experiences or fan communities

## Product Angles

### AI Game Master for Group Play

The AI runs the scenario, adapts the world, and responds to the group in real time.

### Shared Character Roleplay

Users interact with one or more AI characters together inside the same narrative space.

### Multiplayer Simulation Engine

The product becomes a platform for running many kinds of social, training, or entertainment roleplay experiences.

## MVP

- multiple users join the same session
- AI manages the scenario and responds to group actions
- players can speak, type, or choose actions
- world state updates for everyone in the session
- lightweight narrative memory across the session

## Risks

- group coordination and pacing may be hard to get right
- AI consistency becomes more difficult in shared sessions
- needs a clear reason to be multiplayer instead of solo
- moderation and safety may matter quickly if experiences are social and open-ended

## Open Questions

- Is the first wedge entertainment, education, therapy-adjacent, or training?
- Is the experience mostly text, voice, 3D world interaction, or a hybrid?
- Is the AI mainly a character, a narrator, a referee, or all three?
